# 3.8 - User-First Filter Grouping (Matches + Inbox)

## Why the left panel feels too long today

The current filter rail behaves like a single flat checklist. From a user perspective, this forces too much scrolling before they can answer simple questions:

1. "Can I trust this profile?"
2. "Is this person broadly compatible?"
3. "Do finer details matter enough to filter now?"

When "quick decision" and "deep refinement" filters are mixed together, users cannot form a fast mental map. The fix is not more filters; the fix is better sequencing.

---

## Final taxonomy (single recommended approach)

Keep the current UI layout pattern (left rail + drill-down screens), but reorder and group content into a progressive decision flow:

1. `Quick Screen` (tab-contextual)
2. `Core Compatibility`
3. `Life Context`
4. `Education & Career`
5. `Beliefs & Lifestyle`
6. `Detail Refiners`

This order mirrors real behavior: users first reduce noise, then check non-negotiables, then apply narrow refinements.

---

## 1) Quick Screen (tab-contextual, always first)

### Matches (discovery-first)
- `Age & Height` (Free)
- `Photo` (Free)
- `Verified` (Free)
- `Online Status` (Premium)

Why this works: people browsing Matches make split-second shortlist decisions. They want freshness/trust/availability before deep attributes.

### Inbox (triage-first)
- `Photo` (Free)
- `Verified` (Free)
- `Managed by` (Free)
- `Online Status` (Premium)

Why this works: Inbox users are evaluating incoming demand. Trust + seriousness signals ("who runs the profile?") are the fastest first-pass triage.

---

## 2) Core Compatibility

- `Marital Status` (Free)
- `Have Children` (Premium; shown only when marital status is previously married)
- `Diet` (Free)
- `Manglik` (Premium)

Why this works: these are high-impact acceptance/rejection criteria and should appear before social or prestige filters.

---

## 3) Life Context

- `Country` (Free)
- `City` (Premium)
- `Country grew up in` (Premium)
- `Residency Status` (Premium)
- `Location` (Premium)
- `Family` (Premium)
- `Family Details` (Premium)
- `Managed by` (Free, also visible in Inbox Quick Screen)

Why this works: users mentally process "where/how life will work" as a cluster (mobility, family structure, practical context).

---

## 4) Education & Career

- `Education` (Free)
- `Employed in` (Premium)
- `Occupation` (Premium)
- `Income` (Premium)
- `College` (Premium)
- `Company` (Premium)

Why this works: broad qualification should be early; prestige-detail filters (`College`, `Company`) should come later because they are high specificity refiners.

---

## 5) Beliefs & Lifestyle

- `Religion` (Free)
- `Mother Tongue` (Free)
- `Community` (Premium)
- `Astro` (Premium)

Why this works: broad cultural alignment first, then finer community/astrology constraints.

---

## 6) Detail Refiners (last cluster)

- `Hobbies` (Premium)
- `Financial Status` (Premium)

Why this works: these are useful, but rarely first-pass filters. Keeping them last reduces perceived complexity for most users.

---

## Free vs Premium strategy (user-trust friendly)

Use this simple rule to avoid "random lock" frustration:

- Free = broad, high-frequency, foundational filters users expect by default.
- Premium = narrow, high-specificity, low-frequency refiners that increase precision.

### Practical entitlement pattern
- Keep at least 1-2 free filters in every major section.
- If a section is mostly premium, leave the section visible but show unlocked-free options first.
- Show lock only at interaction time (tap), not as visual noise across every row.

## Ordering rule inside each group

Within every group, keep this order:
1. Free and broad filters first
2. Premium and narrow filters next
3. Search-heavy filters last

This gives fast scanability and still supports power users without overwhelming new users.

---

## Rollout recommendation

### Phase 1 (must-do)
- Apply the 6-group structure and ordering in both Matches and Inbox.
- Keep all current filter behavior unchanged (only organization changes).
- Use the same group names across both tabs for memory consistency.

### Phase 2 (polish)
- Standardize premium lock messaging across all premium filters.
- Add section-level "x active" chips to reduce the need to re-open groups.
- Track key metrics: filter usage depth, time-to-first-apply, apply-to-conversation conversion.

---

## Final recommendation

Adopt this single taxonomy as the default for both Matches and Inbox. It is user-mental-model aligned (quick trust -> compatibility -> context -> deep refinement), reduces perceived panel length, and preserves premium monetization without making the experience feel cluttered or arbitrarily paywalled.
